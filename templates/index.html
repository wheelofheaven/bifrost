{# §2 — The Working Hypothesis #}

The hypothesis

An advanced human civilization, not a god.

Wheel of Heaven holds, as a working hypothesis, that the Elohim of the older creation traditions were a small advanced human civilization from elsewhere — that they came here, prepared the planet, designed life on Earth, and left a record in the religious texts. The site reads those texts through that hypothesis.

{# §4 — The Cosmic Clock #}

The cosmic clock

Twelve ages. Twenty-six thousand years. One arc.

Earth's axis traces a slow circle in the sky, completing one full cycle every 25,920 years — the precession of the equinoxes. Divided by the twelve constellations of the zodiac, that cycle yields ages of about 2,160 years each. The ancient astronomers preserved this clock in their monuments and myths. The corpus reads the creation traditions across its twelve ages.

{# §6 — How the Project Reads #}

The method

A working hypothesis, not a creed.

The reading is a hypothesis to be tried, not a doctrine to be defended. The corpus reads close to its sources — Raëlian, biblical, Mesopotamian, Vedic, and others — with translations named, parallels labeled, and differences preserved. Every page marks its main claim as direct, inferred, or speculative. Where the evidence shifts, the reading shifts.

  • D irect explicit in a primary source
  • I nferred a reasonable reading of a source
  • S peculative interpretive synthesis or hypothesis
